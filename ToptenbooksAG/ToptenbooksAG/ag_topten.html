A journey into the mysterious world of Agatha Christie

The Detection Club, established in 1930, was an association of British crime writers. Its founding members included renowned authors such as Agatha Christie, Dorothy L. Sayers, and Anthony Berkeley. The club was formed with the aim of promoting the writing of detective fiction and fostering collaboration among its members.

One of its unique features is the initiation ritual, which involves taking a solemn oath to adhere to certain principles of fair play in detective fiction. Over the years, the Detection Club has played a significant role in shaping the genre of detective fiction and continues to be a respected institution in the literary world.

With intricate plots, well-developed characters, and adherence to the principles of fair play in detective fiction, these novels embody the essence of the genre as championed by the club's founding members. Aspiring writers and fans alike can draw inspiration from these timeless classics while also appreciating their significance within the context of the Detection Club's legacy.
